Essential points

  • A defensible AI use begins with a precise professional task, not with a technology looking for a purpose.
  • Before deployment, safeguards must reflect the data involved, system autonomy and consequences of error.
  • After deployment, evaluation must include quality, verification workload, skills, autonomy and collective work.

One common principle: start from real work

The value of an AI system cannot be inferred from novelty or general benchmark performance. It must be assessed on a situated task, with the people concerned, explicit criteria and the ability to correct or stop the deployment.

Written by Dr Charles Broutin, occupational physician and AI representative for the French Society of Occupational Health.
