In 30 seconds

An AI use only has value in a specific work situation.

  • The right question is not “what can AI do?”, but “which task does it change, for whom and under what conditions?”
  • Before deployment, the data involved, system autonomy and consequences of error determine the required level of vigilance.
  • After deployment, usefulness must be weighed against verification workload, errors, skills and collective effects.

Concrete application · Occupational health

Using a language model as a second reader of occupational-health recommendations.

The article “A concrete AI use case in occupational health” presents the task studied, the protocol, the results and the limitations of this use.

Published, contextualised results

A second reader, never a decision-maker.

A language model is used to flag potential quality issues in occupational-health recommendations. It neither writes the recommendation nor decides in place of the occupational physician.

Two stages · Two different questions

Decide before. Verify afterwards.

The two operational pathways are clearly separated: preparing a use is not the same as observing its real-world effects.

Before deployment02

Control the use before it changes the organisation.

Define the task, choose a level of vigilance and organise a limited, verifiable and reversible pilot.

Level 1Monitoring, synthesis or rewriting from public sources.
Level 2Supervised professional use: risk assessment, training, workplace report or second reading.
Level 3Sensitive data, medical monitoring or a system directly affecting people.

Common principle

Assess AI across the whole work situation.

Usefulness cannot be inferred from the novelty of a tool or its general performance. It must be assessed on a situated task, with data, people, an organisation and observable effects.
