Legal & governance · From use to decision

Govern AI from its real-world use.

Qualify the use, involve the right actors, prepare consultation and authorise — or refuse — a documented, reversible and monitored pilot.

Thomas Cole, The Architect’s Dream, 1840

In 30 seconds

The law does not apply to “AI” in the abstract.

It applies to a specific use, in a particular organisation, involving identifiable people, data, and consequences.

  • The AI Act, GDPR, French labour law, and occupational risk prevention may all apply at the same time.
  • Qualification should come before procurement, piloting, and consultation.
  • Initial compliance does not replace monitoring of effects on real work.

Starting point · 01

Start from the system’s actual authority.

A model name or commercial promise is not enough. Legal qualification starts with four practical questions connected to the activity.

01
Use

Which task is being transformed?

Rewriting, recommending, ranking, allocating, monitoring, or deciding.

02
People & data

Who is affected?

Applicants, workers, patients, professionals — and the data used to describe them.

03
Authority

What can the output produce?

Revisable assistance, guidance, control, or a significant decision.

04
Effects & redress

How can it be challenged or stopped?

Human intervention, traceability, a route for appeal, and a fallback solution.

What this analysis triggers

01A risk classification under the AI Act.
02A data assessment under the GDPR.
03Social dialogue suited to the transformation.
04Occupational risk assessment for health and work.

Obligations · 02

Four frameworks that apply together.

AI Act · current legal timeline

Progressive application.

Dates should be checked again when a project is assessed: implementing texts and future amendments may refine the framework.

1 Aug 2024Entry into force

The regulation became the European reference framework.

2 Feb 2025First rules

Prohibited practices, the AI-system definition, and AI literacy.

2 Aug 2025General-purpose AI

Rules for GPAI models and governance provisions.

2 Aug 2026General application

Most provisions become applicable, subject to the regulation’s exceptions.

Political agreement · 7 May 2026 The Commission reports that the political agreement on the “AI omnibus” moves the application of rules for certain high-risk systems to 2 December 2027 and for systems embedded in regulated products to 2 August 2028. Check formal publication of the amending text before relying on these dates.

Governance · 03

A collective decision, not end-of-process approval.

Each participant contributes a different part of the assessment. Governance makes trade-offs visible, avoids blind spots, and identifies who can suspend use.

Project leadershipDefines the need and owns the decision
Legal & DPOQualify the framework and the data
Worker representativesAssess the transformation of work
Occupational health serviceClarifies effects on work and health
Professionals & managersTest actual use and its limits
Procurement & securityDocument the provider and technical control

Five decision gates

  1. 01
    Demonstrated needA precise work problem, without starting from a tool already selected.
  2. 02
    Qualified useSystem role, data, people, and risk level.
  3. 03
    Organised dialogueInformation and consultation early enough to influence the project.
  4. 04
    Reversible pilotSuccess criteria, warning signals, redress, and fallback solution.
  5. 05
    Documented monitoringObserved effects, incidents, corrections, and a decision to continue or stop.

CSE · make consultation meaningful

Provide a dossier that supports an informed opinion.

A product demonstration is not enough. The dossier should describe the expected transformation of work, affected people, decision-making latitude, and planned monitoring.

Before

Make the project open to scrutiny

  • need and intended uses
  • affected population and tasks
  • alternatives considered
During

Document trade-offs

  • system data and criteria
  • human intervention and redress
  • expected effects on work
After

Return with evidence

  • observed incidents and errors
  • workload, autonomy, and quality
  • corrections or withdrawal

Note: obligations depend on factors including workforce size, the nature of the project, and its effects. This page provides a general method and does not replace legal advice tailored to the situation.
