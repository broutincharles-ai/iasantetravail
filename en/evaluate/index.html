Specialised protocol · article submitted

A high medical score is not enough.

The useful question is not “which model is best?”, but “what does it actually know about the field, which errors does it make and for which tasks might it be worth testing?”

01

A hybrid field

Occupational health combines medicine, labour law, exposure assessment, ergonomics, toxicology, psychosocial risks, work retention and dialogue with workplace stakeholders.

02

A French corpus

The Labour Code, occupational-disease tables, institutional guidance, doctrine and case law are poorly covered by international medical evaluations.

03

Domain-specific errors

An answer may be medically plausible but regulatorily wrong, legally correct but insufficient for prevention, or confuse medical advice with an employer’s decision.

What a benchmark is

A controlled comparison, not a league table.

Several models receive the same questions under the same conditions, and their answers are evaluated using the same rules. This protocol makes it possible to compare knowledge, reasoning, errors and stability.

Its value: identify candidate models and prepare a local evaluation. Its limit: it does not certify safety, compliance or usefulness in real work.

Written by Dr Charles Broutin, occupational physician and AI representative for the SFST.
