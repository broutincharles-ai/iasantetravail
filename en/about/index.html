Professional profile

Occupational physician, educator and author on artificial intelligence at work

Dr Charles Broutin works as an occupational physician at the French National Occupational Health Service, in Paris, where he leads the Training & Methods Unit. His work sits at the intersection of clinical practice, collective prevention, professional education and the analysis of digital transformation at work.

He is the AI representative for the French Society of Occupational Health and co-authors the French-language newsletter AI in Occupational Health with Dr Sara Trinh.

He trained in occupational medicine at the University of Lille and completed a university diploma in artificial intelligence in health in 2023. His occupational-medicine dissertation, defended in 2025 and awarded the Bossons Futé prize, examined the use of large language models to identify quality defects in occupational-health recommendations.

International scope. The website translates experience from French occupational health into broader questions about work design, psychosocial risks, human oversight, evaluation and governance. France-specific rules are identified as such.

Publications

Scientific and professional work

Two publications address the use of large language models and their implications for occupational-health practice and psychosocial risks.

2025 · Psychosocial risks

Large language models and new psychosocial challenges at work

Original French title: Les grands modèles de langage et les nouveaux enjeux psychosociaux au travail

The article analyses the introduction of LLMs into organisations through the dimensions of the Gollac framework, including work intensification, autonomy, skills, collective work and insecurity.

Archives des Maladies Professionnelles et de l’Environnement, 86, 102877.

View the DOI ↗
2026 · Quality of recommendations

Using artificial intelligence to improve occupational-health recommendations

Original French title: L’intelligence artificielle pour renforcer la précision des préconisations médicales

The study evaluates a language model as a supervised second reader for detecting several categories of quality defects, without replacing workplace analysis or the occupational physician’s decision.

Références en Santé au Travail, TF 335, issue 186, June 2026.
